Can We Handle the Truth?


Course Leaders: Tom Naps and Brian Tebbitt
Course Assistant: John Fredell

Welcome to the course home page for the spring term OLLI course Can We Handle the Truth?. The course will meet in Magrath 8 for six Fridays, 12:30-2:00, starting April 11. Feel to contact Tom or Brian anytime you have questions or concerns about the course.

Course description from OLLI catalog: In A Few Good Men, when Tom Cruise famously demands of Jack Nicholson “I want the truth”, he is thunderously rebuked by Nicholson’s “You can’t handle the truth!” What is this thing we call truth? We live in an age surrounded by information and seem increasingly incapable of deciding whether any of it is true. In this co-taught course, we will examine truth, logic, and semantics from two perspectives: philosopher/cognitive scientist (Brian) and mathematician and computer scientist (Tom). Will we reach a consensus or just come away with more questions?

Course outline

  • Week 1 (Tom): Mathematics as the “art” of making elegant logical arguments, that is, proofs. What kinds of truths can we learn from those proofs? Although not necessary, before class I encourage you to watch this lighthearted and enlightening interview of renowned mathematician Edward Frenkel on the old Colbert Report. (Apologies for its quality – copyright issues forced me to splice together segments I found on the Internet Archive.)
    • Week 1 slides
    • In the first week’s class, we talked about a new proof of the Pythagorean Theorem that was created by two high school students in New Orleans. Here is a link to a Scientific American report about them and what makes their new proof unique.
  • Week 2 (Brian): The modern philosophical discussion of truth has its roots in the writings of Plato and Aristotle. A tour through various theories of truth: correspondence, coherence, redundance, deflationary, and pragmatic. We will also take a look at the so-called “Liar’s Paradox”. Week2 slides
  • Week 3 (Tom): Gödel and Turing reveal the truth about proof. Two fundamental truths in a realm of paradoxes. Are their results a glass half full or half empty? Before class, try to watch this Numberfile talk by Marcus duSautoy. Week 3 slides
  • Week 4 (Brian): Metalogical discussion of general logical inference: deduction, induction, and abduction. The “problem of induction” will also be discussed. Week4 slides
  • Week 5 (Tom): Truth as crowd-sourcing. Smoke and mirrors done with probabilistic reasoning. Before class, try to watch this video on Lewis Carroll’s Pillow Problem. Week 5 slides
  • Week 6 (Brian): Welcome to the Philosophy of Language! And particularly the philosophical study of linguistic meaning. Do words ‘have’ meanings. If so, how? From where is the meaning of our spoken and written words derived? Week 6 slides

Slides for each week will be posted as soon as they are finalized. Both Brian and Tom adhere to the “just in time” teaching strategy, tinkering with the minutiae until we walk in the door. So the truth is that getting the slides posted early is unlikely. 😀
